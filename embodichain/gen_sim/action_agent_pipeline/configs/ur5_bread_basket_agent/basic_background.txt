The scene comes from the exported 1780562836_gym_project mesh environment.

This configuration directory is for the UR5BreadBasket task. The current robot
is a dual-UR5 composite robot with DH_PGI_140_80 parallel grippers.

The robot is a dual-UR5 composite robot with two parallel
grippers:
- left_arm is the UR5 outside the left side of the table's near long edge.
- right_arm is the UR5 outside the right side of the table's near long edge.

Both UR5 bases are on the same long side of the table and face inward toward
the central wicker_basket. The bases are intentionally kept outside the table
edge to avoid initial robot-table contact.

The interactive objects are:
- left_bread_roll: the bread mesh initially on the negative-x side
  (source object bread_2).
- right_bread_roll: the bread mesh initially on the positive-x side
  (source object bread_1).
- wicker_basket: the target basket near the center of the table
  (source object basket_3).

Additional passive clutter objects are present on the far side of the table:
- scope: a static high-poly clutter mesh, not a target and not a container.
- popcorn_cup: a static high-poly clutter mesh, not a target and not a
  container.

Do not manipulate the passive clutter objects. They are included only to make
the scene more realistic while leaving the bread-to-basket task unchanged.

The nominal task starts with simultaneous dual-arm grasping. The left UR5 must
grasp left_bread_roll while the right UR5 grasps right_bread_roll in the same
graph edge. After both bread rolls are held, the left UR5 places
left_bread_roll into wicker_basket, releases it, and retreats upward. The next
graph edge is a parallel handoff: the left UR5 returns to its initial pose while
the right UR5 simultaneously moves its already-grasped right_bread_roll to the
high staging pose above wicker_basket. The right UR5 then lowers and releases
right_bread_roll, retreats upward, and returns to its initial pose. To change
the insertion order later, edit the task prompt sequence and keep the same
atomic action API.

The wicker_basket area is a shared workspace. After a UR5 releases a bread
roll, it should retreat upward before the other UR5 moves to the basket,
otherwise the two arms may collide near the basket. The right UR5 should keep
holding right_bread_roll while the left UR5 performs its placement and upward
retreat. Once that retreat is complete, the right UR5 may move toward the
basket while the left UR5 simultaneously returns to its initial pose; it must
not wait for the left return-to-initial motion to finish.

A bread roll at a high pose above `wicker_basket` is only staged, not placed.
Each arm must lower the held bread roll into the basket release pose and open
the gripper before any return-to-initial motion.

Always plan to the current `wicker_basket` object pose from the environment
config. Do not hard-code basket coordinates in generated graph actions.
